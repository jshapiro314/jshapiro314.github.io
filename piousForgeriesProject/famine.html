Famine Stela -

Description:

The Famine Stela is hieroglyphic inscription located on Sehel Island in the Nile near Aswan in Egypt. This stela was carved into a granite cliff, and contains 32 columns of text. At the top is a depiction of three Egyptian deities being presented offerings from King Djoser. The inscription tells the story of a 7 year famine in Egypt. King Djoser asks his high priest Imhotep for help, and Imhotep prays to the god Khnum at Elephantine. The Nile floods ending the drought, and King Djoser issues a decree in which he orders Khnumâ€™s temple to be restored. Additionally, the temple of Khnum at Elephantine is given the land between Aswan and Takompso and receives a share of the imports from Nubia.

Purported Time Period:

Around 2670 BCE

Actual Time Period:

Ptolemaic period (305-30 BCE)

Provenance:

As the stela is carved into a natural granite cliff, it has not moved from its original location on Sehel Island.

Current Location:

Sehel Island

Evidence of Forgery

After studying the language and layout of the inscription, most experts agree that the stela can be dated to the Ptolemaic period. Since the stela purports to be from the time of Djoser, it is clear that it is likely a forgery.

Profile of Likely Forger

By studying the writing and identifying the motivations, we can make a reasonable guess regarding who was behind this forgery. While the majority of the text simply tells a story about King Djoser, the end highlights the importance of the temple at Elephantine. This temple, which had kept a relatively low profile, suddenly has attention drawn to itself. It is given vast tracts of land and a portion of the imports of Nubia. For this reason it seems likely temple priests forged the stela. Additionally, during the Ptolemaic time there were numerous religious groups fighting for power and influence. This artifact may have been created to legitimize the temple and control the Elephantine region.

Significance

The Famine Stela is a unique piece for two reasons. First, the size of this artifact is massive, and it would have taken a long time to create. That means this forgery likely made by those of both power and wealth. Second, the main motivation of the forgery may not have been financial gain. Instead, this acts an example of a forgery used to legitimize power and control the masses.
