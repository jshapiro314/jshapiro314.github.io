The Donation of Constantine -

Description:

The Donation of Constantine claims to be a document commissioned by Emperor Constantine I of the Roman Empire. It describes Constantine’s conversion to Christianity after being cured of his leprosy by Pope Sylvester I. In a show of appreciation, Constantine gifts the Catholic church with the entire western Roman Empire.

Purported Time Period:

4th century CE

Actual Time Period:

Around 8th century CE

Provenance:

This document had been circulating around in the Catholic church since about the 8th century AD, but was first cited by Pope Leo IX in the 11th century CE. The oldest known copy of the Donation of Constantine has been dated back to the 9th century CE.

Current Location:

There are no known copies of the document accessible today.

Evidence of Forgery

In the 15th century, Lorenzo Valla, a scholar with extensive knowledge of Classic Latin published his work De falso credita et ementita Constantini Donatione declamatio, which declared the Donation a forgery. He appealed first to a sense of logic, to say that Constantine, a powerful emperor, would never give away his hard earned land, and that Sylvester would not be comfortable accepting such a large donation. The historical record shows no evidence of the donation ever having occurred, despite its momentous nature. Valla’s knowledge of Classical Latin also allowed him to expose the later origin of the piece. The Donation had obvious philological anachronisms that dated the Latin to a much later 8th century CE. Besides this, the Latin errors present in the document were not typical of good scribes, like those who would write for the emperor. All evidence points to the fact that this donation never occurred and is the work of someone centuries removed from the reign of Constantine.

Profile of Likely Forger

This document was likely created by someone within the church hoping to enhance the power of the papacy. Many suggest the document was forged during the reign of Pope Stephen II, hoping to establish precedent for a donation from Pepin, King of the Franks.

Significance

The Donation of Constantine was first cited by Pope Leo in the 11th century AD and was used to assert papal power. In the 13th century it was repeatedly cited to legitimate the papacy’s claim to land in the west. Territorial conflicts and conflicts between the church and secular powers were brewing, and the Donation gave the church an edge in debates about who was to rule certain territories. Lorenzo Valla’s evidence of the forgery of the donation in 15th century AD was initially repressed by the Church, it has since become one of the most notorious “pious” forgeries.
